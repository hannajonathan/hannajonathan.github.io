Anti-Aliasing Gridding Functions

National Radio Astronomy Observatory (Summer 2025)

In aperture synthesis, visibilities, representing the Fourier component of the sky brightness distribution, are measured and accumulated to form a UV plane. To obtain the sky brightness, a fast Fourier transform (FFT) is performed on the visibilities. The FFT algorithm expects a uniformly sampled input. To satisfy this requirement, the visibility data must be interpolated onto an evenly spaced grid using a gridding algorithm. The most commonly used gridding algorithm is convolutional resampling, in which each visibility is convolved with a "gridding convolution function" (GCF).

This summer, I worked on utilizing five alternative GCFs that showed potential for stronger anti-aliasing properties compared to the zeroth order prolate spheroidal GCF used in the Common Astronomy Software Applications: The Blackman-Nuttall, Blackman-Harris, Flat Top, Dolph-Chebyshev, and Kaiser-Bessel apodization functions.

Preliminary results showed that all of these GCF candidates except for Flat Top demonstrated a significant improvement in alias suppression, with an additional benefit of reducing point spread function noise as well. The GCFs all had an increased support size, however. More analysis is currently being done on how significantly this affects the computational cost.
